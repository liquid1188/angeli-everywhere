Hi, I'm Angie.
Angeli, on paper.

Filipino freelance virtual assistant, travel storyteller, and former physical therapist. This is the long version.

— self-portrait, between projects

I trained as a physical therapist before I ever wrote a subject line that made someone smile. The same instinct, really: pay close attention, move with care, follow the work where it wants to go. I just trade clinics for inboxes now, and shoulders for the heavier kind.

For five years I have lived inside the quiet rooms of business — administration, operations, client support, executive assistance. I learned that good work is mostly noticing: the unfinished thread, the calendar collision waiting to happen, the tone a client uses when they are tired and need someone gentler. I'm the person who notices.

Lately I'm building something of my own. @angeli.everywhere — a creator brand stitched from travel light and slow stories. I film with what I have. I write the way I think: diary-like, a little tender, careful with words. I'm picky about what I make and who I make it with. I will turn down a comedic skit before I turn off my own voice.

Here's what you should know if we work together: I'm warm, but I'm not soft. I send the awkward email. I keep the calendar honest. I tell you when something isn't working. And I show up with cinematic patience — because the best work, like the best travel, is rarely rushed.
